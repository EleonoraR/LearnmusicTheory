Lesson 2: Intervals

An interval is the distance between two notes, and it is one of the most important building blocks in music theory. Understanding intervals will help you form melodies and harmonies.

Types of Intervals

Intervals can be described by their size (number of semitones) and quality (major, minor, perfect, etc.). Here are some basic intervals:

  • Unison: Same note (0 semitones)
  • Minor Second: 1 semitone
  • Major Second: 2 semitones
  • Minor Third: 3 semitones
  • Major Third: 4 semitones
  • Perfect Fourth: 5 semitones
  • Perfect Fifth: 7 semitones
  • Major Sixth: 9 semitones
  • Minor Seventh: 10 semitones
  • Major Seventh: 11 semitones
  • Octave: 12 semitones

How to Identify Intervals

To identify an interval, count the number of letter names (A-G) from the first note to the second note. Then, determine the quality (major, minor, etc.) by the number of semitones between them.

Example:

From C to E is a Major Third (4 semitones).
