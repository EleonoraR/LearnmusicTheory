Lesson 5: Chords and Harmony

What are Chords?

A chord is a group of notes played together. The simplest chords are triads, consisting of three notes: root, third, and fifth.

Types of Triads

  • Major Triad: Root, major third, perfect fifth (e.g., C-E-G).
  • Minor Triad: Root, minor third, perfect fifth (e.g., C-Eb-G).
  • Diminished Triad: Root, minor third, diminished fifth (e.g., C-Eb-Gb).
  • Augmented Triad: Root, major third, augmented fifth (e.g., C-E-G#).

Chord Progressions

Chord progressions are sequences of chords that create a sense of movement in music. Here are some common progressions:

  • I-IV-V-I: Widely used in pop and classical music.
  • ii-V-I: A staple in jazz music.

Exercises

  1. Play the major and minor triads starting from C, D, and G.
  2. Write a simple chord progression using the I-IV-V-I pattern.
