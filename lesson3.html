Lesson 3: Chords and Harmony

Chords are made by stacking intervals on top of each other. They form the harmonic backbone of most music, supporting melodies and adding depth to a piece.

Basic Chords

There are three main types of chords:

  • Major Chord: Consists of a root, major third, and perfect fifth.
  • Minor Chord: Consists of a root, minor third, and perfect fifth.
  • Diminished Chord: Consists of a root, minor third, and diminished fifth.

Example: C Major Chord

Notes: C, E, G

This chord is built by stacking a major third (C to E) and a perfect fifth (E to G). It creates a bright, happy sound.

Example: C Minor Chord

Notes: C, Eb, G

The minor chord differs from the major by having a minor third (C to Eb), which gives it a more somber or melancholic sound.

Chord Progressions

In music, chords are often played in sequences known as chord progressions. A common progression in Western music is the I-IV-V progression.

  • I: C major (C, E, G)
  • IV: F major (F, A, C)
  • V: G major (G, B, D)

Try experimenting with these chords in various keys to understand how they work together to create harmony!
