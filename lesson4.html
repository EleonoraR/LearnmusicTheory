Lesson 4: Scales and Modes

Scales are the foundation of music, providing the notes that form melodies and harmonies. Modes are variations of scales that add unique flavors to your music.

What is a Scale?

A scale is a set of notes ordered by pitch. The most common type of scale is the diatonic scale, which has seven notes per octave.

Major Scale Formula

The major scale is defined by this sequence of whole (W) and half (H) steps:

W-W-H-W-W-W-H

Example: C Major Scale (C, D, E, F, G, A, B)

Minor Scale Formula

The natural minor scale follows this sequence:

W-H-W-W-H-W-W

Example: A Minor Scale (A, B, C, D, E, F, G)

Introduction to Modes

Modes are scales derived from the major scale, starting on different degrees. There are seven modes:

  1. Ionian: Same as the major scale (C to C in C Major)
  2. Dorian: Starts on the 2nd degree (D to D in C Major)
  3. Phrygian: Starts on the 3rd degree (E to E in C Major)
  4. Lydian: Starts on the 4th degree (F to F in C Major)
  5. Mixolydian: Starts on the 5th degree (G to G in C Major)
  6. Aeolian: Same as the natural minor scale (A to A in C Major)
  7. Locrian: Starts on the 7th degree (B to B in C Major)

Practical Example: Dorian Mode

If you play the C Major scale but start and end on D, you are playing the Dorian mode (D, E, F, G, A, B, C).

It has a jazzy and slightly mysterious sound compared to the major scale.

Why Learn Scales and Modes?

  • Understand the building blocks of melodies and harmonies.
  • Explore different musical styles and genres.
  • Improvise and compose music with a broader palette of sounds.

Practice playing scales and modes on your instrument. Start with the major scale, then experiment with different modes to hear their unique flavors!
